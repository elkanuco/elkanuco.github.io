ITIL Practices

ITIL practices are sets of organizational resources designed for performing work or accomplishing objectives. They are the tools of the trade in service management and cover a wide array of processes, functions, and capabilities.

Core Practices for ITIL 4 Foundation

  1. Continual Improvement: Ongoing efforts to improve services, practices, and outcomes for alignment with changing business needs.
  2. Change Enablement: Maximizing successful service/product changes through proper assessment of risks, authorization, and scheduling.
  3. Incident Management: Minimizing negative impact by restoring service operation as quickly as possible following incidents.
  4. Problem Management: Reducing incidents and their impact by identifying and managing causes, workarounds, and known errors.
  5. Service Request Management: Handling pre-defined, user-initiated requests efficiently (e.g., access, information, minor updates).
  6. Service Desk: Capturing demand for requests and incident resolution as the entry point and single point of contact for users.
  7. Service Level Management: Setting and reviewing business-based service level targets and ensuring service delivery aligns with these.
