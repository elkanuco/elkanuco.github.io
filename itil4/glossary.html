Glossary of ITIL 4 Terms

Best Practice
A proven way of working used successfully by multiple organizations.
Change Enablement
Practice to maximize successful service/product changes by managing risks, authorization, and scheduling.
Continual Improvement
Ongoing efforts to align services and practices with changing business needs.
Customer
Defines requirements for a service and is responsible for the outcomes.
Engage
Activity to understand stakeholder needs, maintain transparency, and build stakeholder relationships.
Governance
How an organization is directed and controlled, including decision-making and maintaining alignment.
Incident
An unplanned interruption to a service, or reduction of service quality.
Incident Management
Practice to restore normal service as quickly as possible, minimizing negative impacts.
ITIL Practices
Sets of organizational resources for performing work or accomplishing objectives in service management.
Outcome
A result for a stakeholder, enabled by one or more outputs.
Output
Tangible or intangible delivery of an activity.
Problem
The cause (actual or potential) of one or more incidents.
Problem Management
Practice to reduce the likelihood and impact of incidents by identifying and addressing causes.
Service
A means of co-creating value by facilitating outcomes customers want to achieve, without the customer managing specific costs/risks.
Service Consumer
The customer, user, or sponsor who consumes or pays for the service.
Service Desk
Practice and function to capture demand for incidents/requests and be the primary contact point for users.
Service Level Management
Practice to ensure service delivery meets agreed targets aligned to business goals.
Service Request
User-initiated request for a pre-defined service action (e.g., access, information).
Sponsor
Person who authorizes the budget for service consumption.
User
Person who uses the service.
Utility
What the service does (fit for purpose).
Value
Perceived benefits, usefulness, and importance of something; co-created and subjective.
Warranty
Assurance that a product/service will meet agreed requirements (fit for use).
