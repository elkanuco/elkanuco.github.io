Guiding Principles

The seven guiding principles of ITIL 4 provide universal recommendations for organizations to act effectively and efficiently in all circumstances, regardless of changes in goals, strategies, or work types.

  1. Focus on Value – Know what is valuable to your stakeholders and prioritize actions that create value.
  2. Start Where You Are – Use existing resources, services, processes, and knowledge as a foundation for improvement.
  3. Progress Iteratively with Feedback – Achieve goals incrementally, learning and adapting at every step.
  4. Collaborate and Promote Visibility – Engage the right stakeholders and foster transparency across teams and activities.
  5. Think and Work Holistically – Recognize the interconnectedness of all service components and organizational units.
  6. Keep It Simple and Practical – Eliminate unnecessary complexity and focus on practical outcomes and value.
  7. Optimize and Automate – Continuously look for opportunities to streamline ways of working and leverage automation where suitable.

These principles should be consistently applied and adapted to the specific needs and context of each organization.
