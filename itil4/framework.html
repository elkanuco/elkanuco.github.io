The ITIL 4 Framework

Service Value System (SVS) and Four Dimensions Model

ITIL 4 introduces the Service Value System (SVS), which demonstrates how all organizational components and activities work together as a system to enable value creation through IT-enabled services.

  • The ITIL service value chain – Linked activities that deliver services and products to consumers.
  • ITIL practices – Areas of work (like service desk, problem management) that provide the tools of service management.
  • Guiding principles – Universal attitudes for effective and efficient work (e.g., focus on value, keep it simple, iterative progress).
  • Governance – How the organization is directed, controlled, and aligned.
  • Continual improvement – Ongoing efforts to enhance all organizational aspects, from governance to value creation.

The Four Dimensions

  1. Organization and People: Human resources, culture, skills, roles, and communication.
  2. Information and Technology: Information required and the IT/service technologies that support services.
  3. Partners and Suppliers: External and internal entities that provide services, goods, or expertise.
  4. Value Streams and Processes: Activities, workflows, and procedures for service creation and delivery.

These dimensions are integrated for effective and efficient value creation.
