PRINCE2 7th Edition

Complete Guide to Practices, Techniques, and Management Products

PRINCE2 (PRojects IN Controlled Environments) is a process-based project management method that provides a structured framework for managing projects of any size or complexity.

The 7th Edition introduces refined practices, enhanced management approaches, and updated guidance for modern project delivery. This guide covers all seven core practices, their underlying techniques, and the complete set of management products (plans, logs, registers, and reports).

Seven Core Practices

1. Business Case

Ensures continuous business justification through structured evaluation of costs, benefits, and risks. Includes Business Case Management Technique and Benefits Management Approach.

2. Organizing

Defines roles, responsibilities, and stakeholder engagement strategies. Covers Team Structure Design, Stakeholder Analysis, and Communication Planning.

3. Plans

Product-Based Planning approach defining deliverables before activities. Includes Project Plans, Stage Plans, and Team Plans with estimation and scheduling techniques.

4. Quality

Quality planning, control, and assurance activities. Covers Quality Review Technique, Product Descriptions, Quality Management Approach, and Quality Register management.

5. Risk

Systematic risk management through identification, assessment, planning, implementation, and communication. Includes Risk Register and Risk Management Approach.

6. Issues

Formal handling of five issue types: Request for Change, Off-Specification, Problem/Concern, Business Opportunity, and External Event. Includes Configuration Management.

7. Progress

Monitoring and control through stage boundaries and exception management. Includes Earned Value Management, tolerance setting, and progress reporting.
