PRINCE2 Management Approaches

Management Approaches are project-specific documents that define HOW each practice will be implemented. They are created during Initiating a Project and approved by the Project Board as part of the Project Initiation Documentation (PID). Each approach tailors PRINCE2 guidance to the project's specific context, constraints, and environment.

Core Management Approaches

Benefits Management Approach

Aspect Details
Linked Practice Business Case
Owner Executive
Purpose Define how and when project benefits will be measured, tracked, and realized
Key Contents • Benefit categories and types
• Baseline and target measures
• Measurement methods and KPIs
• Benefits owners and responsibilities
• Timing of benefit realization
• Post-project review schedule
• Tolerances for benefit achievement

Approach Lifecycle

Creation and Approval

Stage Activity
Starting Up a Project (SU) Consider which approaches are needed; gather existing standards
Initiating a Project (IP) Create all required management approaches; tailor to project context
IP - Approval Project Board approves approaches as part of PID
Throughout Project Follow approved approaches; update only if fundamentally necessary
Updates Changes require Project Board approval via change control

Tailoring Considerations

Factor Impact on Approaches
Project Size Small: combine approaches, lighter documentation; Large: detailed separate approaches
Complexity Simple: standard templates; Complex: customized approaches with specialist input
Risk Profile High risk: more detailed risk and quality approaches; Low risk: streamlined approaches
Organizational Standards Align approaches with corporate policies, standards, and existing frameworks
Regulatory Environment Heavily regulated: detailed compliance sections in approaches; Light regulation: focus on efficiency
Stakeholder Landscape Many stakeholders: detailed communication approach; Few: simpler communication plan
Programme Context Part of programme: align with programme approaches; Standalone: independent approaches

Integration of Approaches

Management approaches work together to provide comprehensive project control:

  • Quality Management Approach defines quality standards that feed into Product Descriptions created via Plans
  • Risk Management Approach guides identification of risks that may become Issues requiring Change Management
  • Benefits Management Approach defines measures tracked in Benefits Register and reported in Progress reports
  • Communication Management Approach defines how all other approaches are communicated to stakeholders per Organizing practice
  • Commercial Management Approach influences Plans (supplier schedules) and Quality (supplier deliverables)
  • Sustainability Approach impacts Business Case (sustainability benefits) and Quality (environmental standards)
  • Digital/Data Approach supports all practices through tools, data governance, and reporting infrastructure
