Business Case Practice

The Business Case practice ensures continuous business justification throughout the project lifecycle. It establishes why the forecast effort and investment is worthwhile and provides the basis for project approval and ongoing investment decisions.

Business Case Management Technique

The Business Case Management Technique consists of four distinct stages that ensure continuous business justification:

1. Develop Stage

Aspect Details
When Starting Up a Project (SU) → Initiating a Project (IP)
Who Executive (owner) with Project Manager support
Activities • Identify and explore all feasible options
• Assess viability (can it be done?)
• Assess desirability (should it be done?)
• Assess achievability (will it be done?)
• Estimate costs and timescales
• Identify benefits and risks
• Compare options and select preferred approach
Output Outline Business Case (SU) → Full Business Case (IP)

2. Check Stage

Aspect Details
When At stage boundaries (Managing a Stage Boundary - SB) and ad-hoc reviews
Who Project Board (approval) with Executive leading review
Activities • Verify assumptions remain valid
• Check external environment hasn't changed
• Review forecast vs. actuals
• Assess if project remains justified
• Confirm alignment with corporate strategy
• Decision: continue, modify, or close
Triggers Stage boundaries, exception situations, significant external changes, major risks materializing

3. Maintain Stage

Aspect Details
When Throughout project - continuously during Controlling a Stage (CS)
Who Project Manager maintains, Executive reviews and approves updates
Activities • Update costs with actual expenditure
• Revise forecasts based on progress
• Update risk assessments
• Adjust timescales if needed
• Re-evaluate benefits realization timeframes
• Document changes and impacts
Formal Updates End of each management stage (SB process) and project closure (CP process)

4. Confirm Stage

Aspect Details
When Closing a Project (CP) and post-project reviews
Who Project Manager documents, Executive confirms, Corporate/Programme reviews benefits
Activities • Confirm expected benefits achieved (or on track)
• Document actual vs. forecast performance
• Identify lessons for future business cases
• Hand over to operational teams for benefits tracking
• Schedule post-project benefit reviews
Output End Project Report with benefits confirmation and Benefits Review Plan

Investment Appraisal Techniques

Net Present Value (NPV)

Aspect Description
Purpose Calculate the present value of future cash flows minus initial investment
When Used Develop stage of Business Case Management Technique
Formula NPV = Σ (Cash Flow / (1 + r)^t) - Initial Investment
Decision Rule NPV > 0 = financially viable; NPV < 0 = not viable
Advantages Accounts for time value of money; considers all cash flows; provides absolute value

Return on Investment (ROI)

Aspect Description
Purpose Measure the gain or loss generated relative to investment cost
When Used Comparing multiple investment options in Develop stage
Formula ROI = ((Net Benefit - Cost) / Cost) × 100%
Decision Rule Higher ROI indicates better investment efficiency
Advantages Simple to calculate and communicate; enables comparison across projects

Payback Period

Aspect Description
Purpose Determine how long it takes to recover the initial investment
When Used Organizations with cash flow constraints or high-risk environments
Calculation Time until cumulative cash flows equal initial investment
Decision Rule Shorter payback period = lower risk; must meet organizational threshold
Limitations Ignores cash flows after payback; doesn't account for time value of money

Benefits Management Approach

Purpose and Contents

Section Description
Scope Defines which benefits are included and excluded from project scope
Benefit Categories Financial, operational, strategic, customer satisfaction, compliance
Measurement Methods How each benefit will be quantified and verified (KPIs, metrics, surveys)
Baseline Measures Current state measurements before project delivery
Target Measures Expected benefit levels post-project with timescales
Tolerances Acceptable variance from target benefit levels
Roles & Responsibilities Who tracks benefits (often operational managers), who approves, reporting lines
Timing When benefits will be realized and when reviews will occur
Review Schedule Post-project benefit review plan with specific dates

Ownership and Lifecycle

Aspect Details
Owner Executive
Created Initiating a Project (IP)
Updated End of each stage (SB) if benefit expectations change; at project closure (CP)
Approved By Project Board as part of Project Initiation Documentation
Post-Project Handed to operational management or programme for ongoing benefit tracking

Related Management Products

Business Case Document

Aspect Details
Owner Executive
Created Outline in Starting Up a Project (SU); Full version in Initiating a Project (IP)
Updated Formally at end of each stage (SB) and at project closure (CP); informally throughout CS
Contains • Executive summary
• Reasons (why project is needed)
• Business options (with rationale for selection)
• Expected benefits (quantified)
• Expected dis-benefits
• Timescale
• Costs
• Investment appraisal
• Major risks

Benefits Register

Aspect Details
Owner Project Manager (maintained throughout); handed to operational teams post-project
Created Initiating a Project (IP)
Updated Continuously as benefits are realized or forecasts change
Contains • Benefit identifier
• Benefit type and category
• Benefit owner
• Baseline measure
• Target measure
• Actual measure
• Date expected
• Date realized
• Status

Integration with Other Practices

Practice Integration Point
Risk Major risks documented in Business Case; risk impacts can invalidate justification
Plans Project Plan timescales and costs feed Business Case; benefits realization timeline influences planning
Quality Quality failures may delay benefits realization; quality costs impact business case viability
Progress Progress reports include business case status; exceptions trigger business case review
Issues Significant issues may require business case re-evaluation and approval
