PRINCE2 Foundation Exam Preparation

This page focuses on commonly confused concepts and key distinctions that appear in PRINCE2 Foundation V7 exam questions. Each section addresses specific learning gaps identified through practice questions.

People Element and Principles Connection

Manage by Stages and People

KEY EXAM POINT: When reviewing how team members relate to each other and if people are performing in their roles, this applies the "Manage by Stages" principle, NOT "Tailor to suit the project".

Concept Explanation
Why Manage by Stages? Stages often mark a change in influencers and key relationships. They are good points to review how the project is planned to deliver, ensuring it remains aligned with how people interact with each other and the wider organizational ecosystem.
Stage Boundaries for People Stage boundaries provide a more controlled way to review the skills needed for the next delivery stages and make changes, even to the Project Board composition.
Team Relationships Regularly reviewing how team members relate to each other is part of managing by stages because these relationships often shift between stages.

Business Case Management - Key Concepts

Continued Business Justification Principle

KEY EXAM POINT: Creating individual business cases for each customer and supplier supports the "Ensure Continued Business Justification" principle.

Concept Details
Why Multiple Business Cases? Customers and suppliers have their own business justifications for participating in the project and may need their own business cases
Benefit Providing tailored justifications ensures that the project's value and objectives remain relevant to all parties throughout its lifecycle
Commercial Context Each stakeholder (customer, supplier) needs to see how the project justifies their investment and participation

Business Case Maintenance

KEY EXAM POINT: The business case is maintained by updating it during each management stage as actual costs are incurred, not just at stage boundaries.

When How Updated
During Each Stage Business case updated with actual costs incurred, progress data, products delivered
At End of Stage Project Manager provides updated business case with progress data and latest forecasted benefits and performance targets
Continuous Process Not just updated at stage boundaries - maintained throughout stage execution

Aligning Products to Business Objectives

KEY EXAM POINT: The Senior User ensuring outputs are utilized to achieve outcomes and realize benefits is "Aligning products to business objectives and tolerances", not planning benefit reviews.

Concept Explanation
For Benefits to be Realized Outcomes must be achieved, which means outputs from the project are used as intended, new capability is defined, and barriers to use are identified
Common Problem Many projects produce products that were never fully used or organizational changes that were never fully embedded
Senior User Role Ensures outputs are effectively utilized (not just delivered) to achieve desired outcomes

Communication Management

Effective Communication Practices

KEY EXAM POINT: Good communication means taking time to listen to pockets of resistance to the project, as these are useful indicators of where communications should be focused.

Practice Why Important
Listen to Resistance Pockets of resistance are useful indicators of where communications should be focused (but not the ONLY communication)
Multiple Formats Provide information in multiple formats that people can share easily and provide multiple pathways to give feedback
Not Just Reporting Insufficient to rely solely on periodic reporting to determine how a project is progressing
Balanced Approach Address resistance but don't spend ALL time on it - maintain communication with supporters too

Communication Management Approach Purpose

KEY EXAM POINT: The Communication Management Approach purpose is to define how the project team intends to obtain responses from stakeholders, not just to inform them.

Change Management vs Change Control

Critical Distinction

KEY EXAM POINT: Change Management = how organizations transition from current state to target state (organizational change). Change Control = process for managing changes to project baselines (part of issue management).

Term Definition Scope
Change Management The means by which an organization transitions from the current state to the target state Organizational perspective - all projects result in CHANGE
Change Control The process by which changes that may affect the project baseline are identified, assessed, and then approved, rejected, or deferred Project management perspective - managing baseline changes (part of issue management)
Benefits Tolerance The permissible deviation in the benefit performance targets allowed before escalation NOT change management or change control

Roles and Responsibilities

Activities Outside the Project Team

KEY EXAM POINT: Integrating new products into their area of business and changing ways of working is carried out by people OUTSIDE the project team, not within it.

Activity Who Performs
Integrating new products Outside project team - business operations staff
Changing ways of working Outside project team - people in business areas affected
Upskilling staff Outside project team - ongoing operational activity
Controlling requirements Within project team - Project Manager responsibility
Setting tolerances for work packages Within project team - Project Manager activity

Management Products - Definitions

Benefits Lifecycle Terms

KEY EXAM POINT: Capability = the complete set of project outputs required to deliver an outcome (NOT just "output").

Term Definition Example
Output The tangible or intangible deliverable of an activity. Specialist products that will be used to enable change A single deliverable like a report or system component
Capability The COMPLETE SET of project outputs required to deliver an outcome All outputs together that create new organizational ability
Outcome The result of the change, normally affecting real-world behavior or circumstances Staff using new system to work differently
Benefit The measurable improvement resulting from an outcome perceived as an advantage 20% cost reduction from new process
Dis-benefit The measurable decline resulting from an outcome perceived as negative Temporary productivity loss during transition

Project Mandate vs Project Brief vs Business Case

Product Source Process Content Level
Project Mandate Provided by the business Pre-project Sets expectations and constraints
Project Brief Created from Project Mandate Starting Up a Project (SU) Contains Outline Business Case
Business Case Developed from Project Brief Full version in Initiating a Project (IP) Detailed justification with costs, benefits, risks

Project Approach

KEY EXAM POINT: The Project Approach defines HOW to deliver the selected business option and forms part of the Project Brief (created during SU).

Aspect Details
Created Starting Up a Project (SU)
Part of Project Brief (SU), reviewed in IP
Owner Project Manager
Updated Reviewed and may be refined during Initiating a Project (IP)
Purpose Documents the solution development approach - HOW the project will deliver its products
Includes • Delivery methods (Waterfall, Agile, hybrid, or other approaches)
• Tools and techniques to be used
• Standards and procedures to follow
• Development environment and infrastructure
• Team organization and ways of working
• Location strategy (single site vs multi-site)
• Operational environment considerations
Key Exam Point Project Approach defines HOW products will be delivered, while Product Descriptions define WHAT will be delivered. Vs Business Case (WHY).

Work Package Description vs Team Plan

KEY EXAM POINT: To oversee work when acting as Team Manager, use Work Package Description, NOT Team Plan. Work Package is used to pass responsibility formally.

Product Purpose Used By
Work Package Description Describes how one or more products will be produced and delivered. Used to pass responsibility formally to Team Manager or team member Project Manager → Team Manager/Team Member
Team Plan Optional detailed plan created by Team Manager for internal team use Team Manager (internal to team)
Stage Plan Detailed plan for current management stage Project Manager for stage control

Project Log (NEW Concept)

KEY EXAM POINT: The Project Log is dynamic and contains current and historic record of project activities and progress, capturing lessons, products, quality, risk information.

Product Purpose
Project Log Maintains live record of project activities and progress - captures wide range including issues, risks, lessons, products, quality
Highlight Report Point-in-time summary report to Project Board (not historical)
Risk Register Specific to risks only
Change Management Approach Static approach document, not activity log

Management Approaches Created in IP

Two key approaches that project must comply with (created at start of IP):

  • Quality Management Approach
  • Sustainability Management Approach (NEW in 7th Edition)

Plus 7 others: Benefits, Communication, Risk, Change, Commercial, Digital/Data Management Approaches, and Plans

Project Context

Commercial Context

KEY EXAM POINT: Working with external suppliers to complete a portion of the project is the Commercial context, not Delivery Method context.

Context Type Definition Example
Commercial Business has entered into commercial relationship with supplier to deliver products. Organization delivering the project (supplier) satisfies needs identified by business (customer) Outsourcing to external vendors
Delivery Method The approach used to deliver products (Waterfall, Agile, hybrid) Agile vs Waterfall choice
Organizational The organizational structure and culture context Matrix vs functional organization
Scale Size and complexity of project Small, medium, large projects

PRINCE2 Processes - Specific Activities

Directing a Project (DP) Activities

KEY EXAM POINT: In DP, the Project Board reviews and approves the Project Initiation Documentation to authorize the project - NOT creating plans (that's CS/SB).

Activity Process Who
Review and approve PID Directing a Project (DP) Project Board decides whether to proceed with rest of project
Create day-to-day plan with Team Managers Managing a Stage Boundary (SB) Project Manager creates Stage Plan near end of current stage
Create and authorize work package Controlling a Stage (CS) Project Manager gives out work to Team Manager
Prepare project for closure (premature) Closing a Project (CP) Project Manager ensures work-in-progress not abandoned, salvages value

Managing Product Delivery (MP) - Products Created

KEY EXAM POINT: Specialist products (the outputs users want) are created during Managing Product Delivery process, NOT management products like approaches or logs.

Product Type When Created Description
Specialist Products Managing Product Delivery (MP) The products users need to realize benefits (project outputs/deliverables)
Stage Plan (initiation) Starting Up a Project (SU) First plan createdin project
Communication Management Approach Initiating a Project (IP) One of nine approach documents created in IP
Lessons Log Starting Up a Project (SU) Created in SU, continually updated throughout project

Quality Management - Key Concepts

Planning Quality Steps

Step Primary Input Key Activities Output
1. Gathering User Inputs Project Product Description (PPD) User quality expectations and acceptance criteria documented in PPD during Starting Up a Project PPD with quality expectations
2. Creating Product Descriptions Project Product Description Translate high-level requirements into detailed specifications with quantitative (numeric) and qualitative (descriptive) measures Product Descriptions with quality criteria
3. Describing Quality Management Approach Product Descriptions Define standards, techniques, roles, and procedures Quality Management Approach document

Important Distinctions

  • Quantitative measures: Numeric terms (e.g., "100 staff", "< 2 seconds response time")
  • Qualitative measures: Descriptive terms (e.g., "easy to use", "professional appearance")
  • When captured: Product requirements are captured when the need for the product is identified, not when ready to develop

Quality Register vs Product Register

Register Purpose What to Review For
Quality Register Tracks all quality management activities Decisions about approved products, quality review results, acceptance status
Product Register Checklist of all products with inspection points Product approval dates, status, and owners

Risk Management - Key Concepts

Risk Terminology

Term Definition Key Point
Threat Uncertain event with negative impact on objectives Not the same as "Problem/Concern" - threats are UNCERTAIN events
Opportunity Uncertain event with positive impact on objectives Relates to Risk practice, not Issues practice
Problem/Concern An issue type for events that have occurred or are certain Part of Issues practice, not Risk practice

Risk Management Procedure - Step Details

Step Key Activity Exam Focus
1. Identify Obtain information about the project to ensure common understanding of specific objectives at risk NOT just finding risks - understanding context and objectives FIRST
2. Assess Estimate probability and impact; prioritize risks Quantitative analysis of identified risks
3. Plan Select appropriate risk responses Know response types (see below)
4. Implement Execute planned responses; monitor effectiveness Action phase
5. Communicate Share risk information with stakeholders Ongoing throughout all steps

Risk Response Strategies

Strategy For Definition Example
Avoid Threats Remove the risk by eliminating the cause Select a new supplier (removes bankruptcy risk cause)
Reduce Threats Lower probability or impact Add extra testing to reduce defect risk
Transfer Threats Share risk with third party Insurance, fixed-price contract
Accept Both Take no action but monitor Low-priority risks within tolerance
Prepare Contingent Plans Threats Plan actions IF risk occurs Backup supplier identified but not engaged yet
Exploit Opportunities Ensure opportunity happens Implement factor that leads to opportunity
Enhance Opportunities Increase probability or positive impact Add resources to capture opportunity faster
Share Opportunities Partner to maximize benefits Joint venture for new market
Reject Opportunities Decide not to pursue Opportunity cost too high

Issues Management - Key Concepts

Issue Management and Change Control Relationship

KEY EXAM POINT: In PRINCE2, issue management encompasses change control. They are NOT separate techniques. Change control is PART OF the 5-step issue management technique.

Five Issue Types (Complete List)

Issue Type Definition Where Documented
1. Problem/Concern Problem: Issue with immediate negative impact requiring resolution
Concern: Potential issue whose impact or timing needs evaluation		 Issue Register → Issue Report (for detailed analysis)
2. External Event Unforeseen development outside project's control Issue Register → Issue Report
3. Business Opportunity Unplanned event presenting potential benefit Issue Report contains impact assessment
4. Request for Change (RFC) Proposal to modify baselined product Issue Register → Issue Report
5. Off-Specification Product missing or not meeting specification Issue Register → Issue Report

Where to Document Impact Assessment

For Document Contains
Business Opportunity (or any significant issue) Issue Report Impact analysis on project baseline, options, recommendations, decision
All issues (initial capture) Issue Register Basic details, type, priority, status
Expected benefits Benefits Register Baseline, target, actual measures
Risks Risk Register Threats and opportunities (uncertain events)

Progress Management - Key Concepts

Types of Control

Type Definition Examples
Time-Driven Control Occurs at predefined periodic intervals Checkpoint Reports, Highlight Reports (regular schedule)
Event-Driven Control Triggered by specific events Exception Reports, End Stage Reports, Completion of PID

Report Types and Purposes

Report Purpose Key Content
Checkpoint Report Team Manager reports to Project Manager on work package progress Period covered, work completed, issues, forecast
Highlight Report Provide Project Board with summary at intervals they define Executive summary, period covered, stage/project tolerance status, key issues/risks
End Stage Report Summarize progress, situation, and ask for Project Board decision on what to do next Executive summary, performance review, follow-on actions, forecast
End Project Report Review how project performed against PID used to authorize it Executive summary, performance review, product review, benefits realized
Issue Report Describe issue's impacts and recommend decision Issue details, impact analysis, options, recommendation, decision

Stage Plan Updates

KEY EXAM POINT: The Stage Plan is created during Managing a Stage Boundary (SB) and is regularly updated with actual progress during Controlling a Stage (CS). The Project Manager uses Checkpoint Reports to update the Stage Plan.

PRINCE2 Processes - Key Concepts

Process Purposes (Commonly Confused)

Process Purpose Key Focus
Starting Up a Project (SU) Ensure prerequisites for initiation are in place Quick check - is it worth detailed planning?
Initiating a Project (IP) Establish solid foundations; define work needed to deliver project product before significant expenditure Detailed planning, all approaches created, resource utilization defined
Directing a Project (DP) Project Board provides overall control while delegating day-to-day management Authority points, business connection maintained
Controlling a Stage (CS) Assign work, monitor work, handle issues, report progress, take corrective action to stay within tolerances Day-to-day management by Project Manager
Managing Product Delivery (MP) Control link between Project Manager and Team Manager Work package acceptance, execution, delivery
Managing a Stage Boundary (SB) Provide Project Board with information to review current stage success and authorize next stage End stage review, plan next stage
Closing a Project (CP) Provide fixed point where acceptance is confirmed; recognize objectives achieved Acceptance, benefits realized, orderly closure

Controlling a Stage - Objectives

  • ✓ Ensure stage's products meet agreed quality expectations
  • ✓ Concentrate on completing work within agreed tolerances
  • Monitor progress and report to Project Board
  • Capture and resolve issues and risks
  • Take corrective action when needed

Directing a Project - Objectives

  • Ensure authority to initiate the project
  • Ensure authority to deliver the project product
  • Provide appropriate management direction throughout project life
  • Ensure project remains viable
  • Maintain business connection to project
  • Ensure authority to close the project
  • Ensure plans for realizing post-project benefits are managed and reviewed

Change Management Context

KEY EXAM POINT: All projects result in a CHANGE from current state to future target state. Change management is how organizations transition from current to target state.

Product-Based Planning - Benefits

KEY EXAM POINT: Product-based planning reduces the risk that important scope aspects will be overlooked by clearly and consistently identifying products and their interdependencies.

Benefit How Achieved
Reduces scope neglect All products explicitly identified in Product Breakdown Structure
Clear deliverables Product Descriptions define exactly what must be delivered
Quality focus Quality criteria defined for each product
Dependencies visible Product Flow Diagram shows interdependencies
