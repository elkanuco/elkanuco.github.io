Risk Practice

The Risk practice provides a systematic approach to identify, assess, and control uncertainty during the project. It ensures that risks are actively managed and that both threats and opportunities are addressed.

Risk Management Procedure (5 Steps)

Step 1: Identify

Aspect Details
When Continuously throughout project - formally during SU, IP, SB, CS, and MP
Who Project Manager facilitates; entire project team participates; stakeholders contribute
Activities • Conduct risk workshops and brainstorming sessions
• Review lessons learned from previous projects
• Analyze project documentation
• Interview stakeholders and specialists
• Examine assumptions and constraints
• Review external environment and dependencies
• Identify both threats and opportunities
Techniques Risk checklists, SWOT analysis, assumption analysis, risk prompt lists
Output List of identified risks (threats and opportunities) added to Risk Register

Step 2: Assess

Aspect Details
When Immediately after identifying risks; regularly reviewed throughout project
Who Project Manager leads assessment; risk owners provide input; specialists evaluate technical risks
Activities • Estimate probability of occurrence (very low to very high)
• Estimate impact on objectives (negligible to very high)
• Calculate risk exposure (probability × impact)
• Assess proximity (when risk might occur)
• Evaluate risk velocity (how quickly impact could escalate)
• Categorize risks by type
• Prioritize risks for treatment
Techniques Probability and Impact Grid, Expected Monetary Value (EMV), Risk Matrix, Qualitative/Quantitative analysis
Output Risk Register updated with assessments and priorities

Step 3: Plan

Aspect Details
When After assessment; revisited when risk status changes
Who Project Manager defines responses; risk owners develop detailed action plans
Activities • Select appropriate response strategy
• Define specific actions for each risk
• Estimate cost and effort for responses
• Assign risk actionees
• Set target dates
• Define success criteria
• Allocate risk budget/contingency
Response Strategies (Threats) • Avoid: Eliminate the risk (change plan, remove cause)
• Reduce: Lower probability or impact
• Transfer: Share risk with third party (insurance, contracts)
• Accept: Take no action but monitor
• Prepare contingency: Plan actions if risk occurs
Response Strategies (Opportunities) • Exploit: Ensure opportunity happens
• Enhance: Increase probability or positive impact
• Share: Partner to maximize benefits
• Reject: Decide not to pursue opportunity
Output Risk Register updated with response plans and risk actionees

Step 4: Implement

Aspect Details
When Throughout project execution - Controlling a Stage (CS) and Managing Product Delivery (MP)
Who Risk actionees execute actions; risk owners monitor progress; Project Manager oversees
Activities • Execute planned risk responses
• Monitor risk status and effectiveness of responses
• Track trigger conditions
• Implement contingency plans when needed
• Measure actual vs. expected results
• Report on risk status
• Update Risk Register with progress
Monitoring Regular risk reviews at team meetings, checkpoint reports, and stage boundaries
Output Risk Register updated with implementation status; risks closed or escalated

Step 5: Communicate

Aspect Details
When Continuously - integrated into all project communications
Who Project Manager communicates to Project Board; Team Managers inform teams; stakeholders kept informed
Activities • Include risk summaries in Highlight Reports
• Report significant risks to Project Board immediately
• Communicate risk status in End Stage Reports
• Share lessons learned about risks
• Keep Risk Register accessible to authorized users
• Escalate risks beyond project tolerance
• Update stakeholders per Communication Management Approach
Communication Channels Highlight Reports, Exception Reports, End Stage Reports, Checkpoint Reports, risk workshops

Risk Management Approach

Purpose and Contents

Section Description
Risk Management Procedure Tailored 5-step process with specific guidance for this project
Risk Appetite & Thresholds Organization's attitude toward risk; acceptable levels of exposure; escalation triggers
Scales & Scoring Probability scale (e.g., 1-5), Impact scale (e.g., 1-5), scoring methodology
Risk Categories Strategic, operational, financial, technical, legal, environmental, etc.
Roles & Responsibilities Who identifies, assesses, owns, and monitors risks; escalation paths
Timing & Frequency When risk reviews occur; triggers for ad-hoc assessments
Tools & Techniques Software tools, templates, risk workshop formats, assessment methods
Reporting Risk reporting formats, frequency, distribution lists
Risk Budget Contingency reserves allocated for risk responses

Ownership and Lifecycle

Aspect Details
Owner Project Manager
Created Initiating a Project (IP)
Updated Only if project risk context fundamentally changes
Approved By Project Board as part of Project Initiation Documentation

Related Management Products

Risk Register

Aspect Details
Owner Project Manager
Created Initiating a Project (IP)
Updated Continuously throughout CS and MP as risks are identified, assessed, and managed
Contains • Risk identifier
• Risk description
• Risk type (threat/opportunity)
• Risk category
• Probability and impact scores
• Proximity
• Risk owner
• Risk actionee
• Response strategy
• Planned actions
• Risk status
• Date raised/closed

Integration with Other Practices

Practice Integration Point
Business Case Major risks documented in Business Case; materialized risks can invalidate justification
Plans Risk responses become activities in plans; risk analysis informs estimates and buffers
Quality Quality failures identified as risks; risk responses may include additional quality checks
Issues Materialized risks become issues; risks may be raised via Issue Register
Progress Risk status reported in progress reports; risk impacts may cause exceptions
