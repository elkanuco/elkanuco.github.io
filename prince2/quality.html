Quality Practice

The Quality practice ensures that the project's products meet their defined requirements and are fit for purpose. It covers quality planning, quality control, and quality assurance activities throughout the project lifecycle.

Planning Quality Activities

Planning quality is a fundamental activity in PRINCE2 that establishes the foundation for how quality will be managed throughout the project. It consists of three key sub-activities:

1. Gathering User Inputs (Project Product Description)

Aspect Details
When Starting Up a Project (SU) → Initiating a Project (IP)
Who Project Manager with Senior User and key stakeholders
Activities • Identify quality expectations from customers/users
• Define acceptance criteria
• Establish quality tolerances
• Document customer quality expectations
Output Project Product Description with quality expectations and acceptance criteria

2. Creating Product Descriptions (Requirements Documents)

Aspect Details
When Initiating a Project (IP) during Product-Based Planning
Who Project Manager (with input from users and suppliers)
Activities • Create Product Breakdown Structure (PBS)
• Draft Product Descriptions for each product
• Define specific quality criteria for each product
• Specify quality methods and skills required
• Identify quality responsibilities
Output Product Descriptions containing: purpose, composition, derivation, format/presentation, quality criteria, quality tolerance, quality method, quality skills, and quality responsibilities
Approval Approved by Project Manager with validation from Senior User and Senior Supplier at end of Initiation Stage

3. Describing the Quality Management Approach

Aspect Details
When Initiating a Project (IP)
Who Project Manager (owner), approved by Project Board
Purpose Defines how quality will be planned, controlled, and assured throughout the project
Updated Only when significant changes to quality approach are needed

Contents of Quality Management Approach

Section Description
Purpose & Scope Objectives, applicability, and purpose of quality management for the project
Quality Standards Corporate/programme standards, external quality systems (ISO/IEC), industry standards to be followed
Tools & Techniques Software tools, procedural tools (Quality Review Technique, testing methods, inspection procedures)
Processes & Timing When and how quality reviews, testing, audits, and inspections will occur; frequency and triggers
Roles & Responsibilities Who performs quality planning, control, and assurance (Project Manager, Team Manager, Project Assurance, reviewers)
Reporting & Records How results are captured (Quality Register), reported (Highlight Reports, End Stage Reports), and archived
Change & Tailoring How updates to the QMA will be handled; tailoring guidance for different product types

Quality Control Activities

Quality Review Technique

The Quality Review Technique is PRINCE2's formal peer-review process for verifying products against their Product Descriptions.

Roles in Quality Review

Role Responsibilities
Chair Facilitates the review meeting, ensures objectivity, manages discussion, records decisions
Presenter Presents the product to reviewers (may be the producer or another team member)
Reviewers Examine the product against quality criteria, identify defects and issues
Producer Created the product, answers questions, responsible for corrections
Administrator Distributes materials, schedules meetings, records actions (optional role)

Quality Review Process Steps

  1. Preparation: Distribute product and Product Description to reviewers in advance
  2. Review Meeting: Chair facilitates structured examination against quality criteria
  3. Recording: Document all defects, questions, and observations
  4. Action List: Assign corrective actions to producer
  5. Follow-up: Verify corrections have been made satisfactorily
  6. Sign-off: Formally approve the product when quality criteria are met

Related Management Products

Quality Register

Aspect Details
Owner Project Manager
Created Initiating a Project (IP)
Updated Continuously throughout Controlling a Stage (CS) and Managing Product Delivery (MP)
Purpose Lists all planned quality activities and records their outcomes
Contains • Quality activity identifier
• Product identifier
• Planned date
• Actual date
• Quality method
• Roles involved
• Result (pass/fail)
• Actions required

Product Register (NEW in PRINCE2 7th Edition)

Aspect Details
Owner Project Manager
Created Initiating a Project (IP)
Updated When products are approved or status changes
Purpose Checklist of all products with inspection points and approval status
Contains • Product identifier
• Product name
• Product status
• Approval date
• Approved by
• Location/reference

Configuration Item Records

Aspect Details
Owner Project Manager (or Configuration Librarian)
Created Initiating a Project (IP)
Updated When products are created, modified, or approved
Purpose Tracks product versions, baselines, and change history under configuration control
