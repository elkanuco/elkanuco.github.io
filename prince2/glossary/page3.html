Business Case Fundamentals

Term Definition
Business Case Documents the business justification for undertaking a project, based on estimated costs against anticipated benefits and offset by associated risks. Outlines how and when expected benefits can be measured.
Outline Business Case The initial version of the business case developed during starting up a project. Has sufficient information to enable the project board to confirm business justification and authorize project initiation.
Full Business Case The detailed version developed during initiating a project. Created after the project is planned in detail and information about costs, timescales, products, risks, and targets becomes clearer.
Business Justification The reason why the project is needed. About understanding the value of a project - the perceived benefits or importance of outcomes in proportion to resources deployed to achieve them.
Value for Money An assessment of whether the project represents an economic and efficient use of resources, providing benefits that justify the investment.

Outputs, Outcomes & Benefits

Term Definition
Output A specialist product delivered by the project. The tangible or intangible products that the project creates.
Outcome The result of change, normally affecting real-world behavior and circumstances. Outcomes are enabled by outputs and lead to benefits realization.
Benefit The measurable improvement resulting from an outcome perceived as an advantage by one or more stakeholders. Realized from the use of project outputs.
Dis-benefit An outcome perceived as negative by one or more stakeholders. Expected consequences of an activity that must be valued and incorporated into investment appraisal.
Capability The ability of an organization or individual to achieve objectives. Outputs create capabilities that lead to outcomes.

Business Objectives & Strategy

Term Definition
Business Objective The measurable outcomes that demonstrate progress in relation to the organization's strategy and to which the project should contribute.
Project Objective The measurable goals that the project must achieve. Should align with and contribute to business objectives.
Project Product What the project must deliver in order to gain acceptance. The aggregate of all specialist products to be delivered by the project.
Project Product Description Describes the project's major products and intended purpose, including user quality expectations and acceptance criteria and methods. Created during starting up, refined during initiating.

Benefits Management

Term Definition
Benefits Management Approach Defines the benefits management actions and benefits reviews to be established to ensure outcomes are achieved and benefits realized.
Benefits Realization The practice of ensuring that the outcomes achieved result in the benefits expected. Often occurs after project closure.
Benefits Tolerance The permissible deviation in the expected benefits without escalating to the next level of management. Part of the seven performance target tolerances.
Benefits Review A review to assess whether actual benefits match forecast benefits. May occur during or after the project.
Post-Project Benefits Review A review conducted after project closure to determine whether anticipated benefits have been realized.

Sustainability

Term Definition
Sustainability Management Approach Defines the actions, reviews, and controls established to ensure sustainability performance targets for the project are achieved.
Environmental, Social and Governance (ESG) Goals Organizational objectives relating to environmental impact, social responsibility, and governance practices. Project sustainability targets should reference and contribute to these.
Sustainability Tolerance The permissible deviation in sustainability performance targets (such as carbon footprint reduction) without escalating to the next level of management.

Business Case Management

Term Definition
Develop (Business Case) To explore options and get the right information upon which investment appraisal decisions can be made.
Check (Business Case) To assess whether the project is (still) worthwhile. Occurs at stage boundaries and when assessing risks and issues.
Maintain (Business Case) To keep the business case updated with actual progress and current forecasts, including forecast benefits.
Confirm (Business Case) To assess whether intended benefits have been (or will be) realized. Mostly occurs after project closure.
Desirable, Viable, and Achievable The three tests that the business case must pass. Desirable means worth doing, viable means can be done cost-effectively, achievable means the organization can deliver it.
