Risk Fundamentals

Term Definition
Risk An uncertain event that, should it occur, will have an effect on the achievement of objectives. Measured by a combination of likelihood and impact. Can be a threat or opportunity.
Threat An uncertain event that would have a negative impact on objectives if it occurred. Negative risks that need to be managed.
Opportunity An uncertain event that would have a positive impact on objectives if it occurred. Positive risks that should be exploited or enhanced.
Uncertainty The state of having limited knowledge where it is impossible to exactly describe the existing state or future outcome. Projects always introduce uncertainty.

Risk Characteristics

Term Definition
Risk Cause The source of the risk, such as the event or situation that causes it. Also called risk drivers. Not risks themselves but potential trigger points.
Risk Event The area of uncertainty in terms of the threat or opportunity that might occur.
Risk Effect The impact that the risk would have on project objectives if the risk materializes.
Risk Likelihood The estimated chance that a risk will occur. Often estimated by considering probability or frequency of occurrence.
Risk Probability The estimated chance that a risk will occur, expressed as a percentage or ratio. Used interchangeably with likelihood.
Risk Impact The estimated effect on objectives should a risk occur. Usually measured against time, cost, quality, scope, benefits, or sustainability.
Risk Proximity How near in time a risk might occur. Helps prioritize risks that need immediate attention.
Risk Velocity How quickly a risk would have an impact on objectives should it occur. Indicates how fast response would need to be.

Risk Assessment

Term Definition
Risk Exposure The degree to which a particular objective is 'at risk'. A neutral concept as exposure can be positive (opportunity) or negative (threat).
Risk Appetite The amount and type of risk that the business is willing to take in pursuit of its objectives. Defines acceptable risk levels.
Risk Tolerance A measurable threshold representing the tolerable range of outcomes for each objective 'at risk', using the same units as for measuring performance for that objective.
Risk Matrix A useful way of plotting the set of risks and their estimations. Represents a situation at a specific point in time and can show trends.
Residual Risk If a threat is reduced rather than removed, the largest realistic likelihood or impact that remains after risk response actions. May require additional responses if significant.
Secondary Risk A risk that arises as a direct result of implementing a risk response. Must be identified, assessed, and controlled like initially identified risks.

Risk Roles

Term Definition
Risk Owner The person assigned to take responsibility for responding to a risk. Responsible for management, monitoring, and control of all aspects of an assigned risk.
Risk Action Owner The person who is the nominated owner of agreed actions to respond to a risk. Also known as the risk actionee. Owns specific actions to address a risk.

Risk Responses

Term Definition
Avoid a Threat / Exploit an Opportunity Making the uncertain situation certain by removing the risk. Achieved by removing the cause of a threat or taking advantage of an opportunity.
Reduce a Threat / Enhance an Opportunity Taking definite action now to change the probability and/or impact of the risk. Mitigation is relevant when discussing threat reduction.
Transfer a Risk Passing part of the risk to a third party (threat or opportunity). Often through insurance or contractual arrangements.
Share a Risk Seeking multiple parties to share the risk on a pain/gain share basis, typically within a supply chain. Different from transfer.
Accept a Risk Taking the chance that the risk will occur. The organization accepts the effect of the risk without active response.
Prepare Contingent Plans A planned set of actions that will be taken for each risk if and when it occurs. Fallback arrangements for threats or opportunities.
Fallback Plan A plan prepared for actions to be taken if a risk occurs and the original response proves inadequate. Contingency planning.

Risk Budget & Management

Term Definition
Risk Budget A sum of money to fund specific management responses to the project's threats and opportunities, including costs of contingent plans should a risk materialize.
Risk Management Approach Describes how risk will be managed on the project, including specific procedures, techniques, standards, and responsibilities to be applied.
Risk Register Maintains a record of identified risks related to the project, including their status and history. Captures and maintains information on all identified threats and opportunities.

Risk Management Procedure

Term Definition
Identify (Risks) Review the plan's context and objectives so that threats and opportunities can be identified. First step in risk management procedure.
Assess (Risks) Prioritize risks and assess the combined risk profile. Evaluate likelihood, impact, proximity, and velocity.
Plan (Risk Response) Decide on risk responses and monitoring arrangements. Select appropriate response strategies for each risk.
Implement (Risk Response) Establish monitoring arrangements and execute actions for priority risks or realized risks. Put responses into action.
Communicate (Risk) Operates in parallel with other steps. Outputs of any risk management step may need to be communicated to stakeholders at any point.

Decision Bias

Term Definition
Decision Bias The inherent tendency for people to adopt mental shortcuts or faulty thinking processes to process situations and make decisions. Natural but can lead to less effective decision-making.
Optimism Bias A mindset that drives people to discount disadvantageous risk, assuming things are likely to be successful for them.
Loss Aversion A mindset that values the avoidance of loss rather than making a gain. Preference for keeping what you have over getting something new.
Groupthink A mindset that makes people value social cohesion in a group more than expressing an alternative point of view or reaching the right decision.
Proximity Bias A mindset where situations that are closer in time are seen as riskier and more important than those in the future.
Risk Culture A supportive risk culture recognizes the importance of considering risk as an important part of decision-making. Understanding and overcoming bias is crucial.
