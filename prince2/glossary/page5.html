Quality Fundamentals

Term Definition
Quality The degree to which a set of inherent characteristics of a product fulfills requirements. Concerned with ensuring products meet user requirements and expectations and enable desired benefits to be realized.
Quality Planning Involves identifying major products, documenting them in terms of user expectations and acceptance criteria. Includes developing the quality management approach and obtaining board approval.
Quality Control Involves implementing the quality management approach, assessing quality issues, and obtaining product acceptance. Done at product level throughout the project.
Quality Assurance Ensuring techniques are sufficient to confirm quality requirements are met. Independent of the project manager and project team, may be part of the user's quality management system.
Requirements The expectations, needs, and desires of a project's stakeholders for the project's products, outcomes, and benefits. Must be prioritized and agreed in a controlled manner.

Quality Specifications

Term Definition
Quality Criteria A description of the quality specifications that a product must meet, and the quality measurements that will be applied by those inspecting the finished product.
Quality Specifications The detailed description of the characteristics or standards required for a product to be fit for purpose. Part of the product description.
User's Quality Expectations A statement about the quality expected from the project product, captured from the users. Forms the basis for project product quality specifications.
Acceptance Criteria A prioritized list of criteria that the project product must meet before the customer will accept it. Defines the level of quality required.
Acceptance Method The means by which acceptance criteria will be measured and confirmed. Describes how acceptance will be demonstrated.

Product Descriptions

Term Definition
Product Description Describes a product's purpose, composition, derivation, and quality specifications. Produced at planning phase as soon as possible after the need for the product is identified.
Purpose (Product) Defines what the product will be used for and who will use it. Explains why the product is needed.
Composition A description of the main parts or components that make up the product. Shows what it contains.
Derivation Identifies the source products from which this product is derived or products needed to create it. Shows dependencies.
Format and Presentation Describes the expected appearance or form of the product. May reference standards or templates to be used.

Quality Management Products

Term Definition
Quality Management Approach Describes the quality techniques and standards to be applied, and the roles and responsibilities for achieving required quality specifications and acceptance criteria during a project.
Quality Register Summarizes all quality management activities that are planned or have occurred. Used by the project manager and project assurance for reviewing progress.
Product Register Lists all products required of a plan and the status of those products. Tracks which products exist, their current state, and who is responsible for them.
Configuration Item Record A record that describes the status, version, and variant of a configuration item, and changes made to it. Used for configuration management.

Quality Methods & Techniques

Term Definition
Quality Review A quality review technique whereby stakeholders of the project product evaluate a product for defects with a view to approving it or agreeing necessary remedial actions.
Quality Inspection A formal examination of a product to assess whether it meets its quality criteria. May involve testing, measuring, or reviewing.
Quality Audit An independent check that quality processes have been followed correctly. Part of quality assurance activities.
Quality Testing The process of exercising software or a system with the intent of finding defects and verifying it meets requirements.
Approval Formal confirmation that a product is complete and meets its quality criteria. Provides authority to use the product or to proceed to next phase.

Quality Management Procedure

Term Definition
Gathering User Inputs The first step in quality management: understanding user needs and capturing quality expectations that will define acceptance criteria.
Describing Quality Management Approach Step in quality management defining how quality will be managed, including techniques, standards, roles, and responsibilities.
Controlling Quality Step involving implementing the quality management approach, performing quality checks, and recording results throughout product development.
Accepting Products The final quality management step where acceptance of project products transfers ownership or responsibility from project/supplier to project board on behalf of users.

Configuration Management

Term Definition
Configuration Management Technical and administrative activities concerned with the creation, maintenance, controlled change, and quality control of products and their components.
Configuration Item An entity that is subject to configuration management. May be a component of a product, a product, or a set of products.
Baseline A reference point representing an agreed version of a product or set of products at a particular point in time. Changes to baselines must be controlled.
Version An iteration of a product that may be a draft, interim, or final form. Each version is numbered or labeled for identification.
Variant A version of a product that coexists with other versions, typically to meet different requirements or contexts.
