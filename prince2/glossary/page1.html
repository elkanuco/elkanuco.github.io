Project Fundamentals

Term Definition
Project A temporary organization created for the purpose of delivering one or more business products according to an agreed business case. Characterized by being temporary, having defined start/end dates, cross-functional teams, unique circumstances, and introducing uncertainty.
PRINCE2 PRojects IN Controlled Environments. A globally recognized project management method with over a million certified professionals working in 150 countries. Based on seven principles, seven practices, and seven processes.
Project Management The planning, delegating, monitoring, and control of all aspects of the project and the motivation of those involved to achieve the project objectives within expected performance targets for time, cost, quality, scope, benefits, sustainability, and risk.
Business As Usual (BAU) The normal ongoing operations of an organization. Projects differ from BAU by being temporary, unique, introducing change, and involving uncertainty.
Project Context The environment and circumstances in which the project operates, including organizational context, commercial relationships, delivery methods, sustainability requirements, and project scale.

The Seven PRINCE2 Principles

Term Definition
Ensure Continued Business Justification Principle stating there must be a justifiable reason for starting the project, and the justification must remain valid throughout the project lifecycle. The business justification is documented in the business case.
Learn from Experience Principle stating that lessons must be sought, recorded, and acted upon throughout the project. Everyone involved must actively seek lessons rather than wait for someone else to provide them.
Define Roles, Responsibilities, and Relationships Principle stating that everyone must have agreed roles and responsibilities, know what to do, and what to expect from others. The team initiates and builds relationships with internal and external stakeholders.
Manage by Stages Principle stating that a PRINCE2 project is planned, monitored, and controlled on a stage-by-stage basis. Management stages are separated by decision points made by the project board.
Manage by Exception Principle stating that a project defines tolerances for each objective. Issues outside agreed tolerances are called exceptions and escalated to the appropriate level. Ensures decisions are made by the right people at the right time.
Focus on Products Principle stating that a PRINCE2 project focuses on the definition and delivery of products, particularly their quality requirements. Product descriptions provide the means to determine effort, dependencies, and schedules.
Tailor to Suit the Project Principle stating that PRINCE2 must be tailored to suit the project's size, environment, complexity, importance, capability, and risk. Tailoring is proactive and must maintain the integrity of the seven principles.

Integrated Elements

Term Definition
Principles The seven mandatory guiding obligations that provide a framework of good practice. All seven must be applied for it to be a PRINCE2 project.
People Those working on the project and affected by it. Understanding people's needs, capabilities, and motivations is crucial to how the project is established and managed.
Practices The seven aspects of project management that must be applied consistently throughout the project: business case, organizing, plans, quality, risk, issues, and progress.
Processes The seven stages of the project lifecycle that describe activities performed at different management levels: starting up, directing, initiating, controlling a stage, managing product delivery, managing a stage boundary, and closing.
Tailoring The appropriate application of PRINCE2 to a specific project, considering context, scale, delivery method, complexity, importance, capability, and risk. The project manager recommends tailoring; the project board approves it.

Performance Targets

Term Definition
Time Performance target relating to the timescales within which the project must deliver. Managed to avoid overruns and customer dissatisfaction.
Cost Performance target relating to the financial expenditure. Managed to avoid overspending or missing opportunities to cut costs.
Quality Performance target relating to ensuring products are fit for purpose and meet customer expectations. Quality specifications define the required standards.
Scope Performance target determining exactly what the project will deliver. Agreement on what is and is not within scope is essential. Delivering beyond scope is a common source of delays and overspend.
Risk Performance target relating to identifying and managing potential threats and opportunities. Managing risk keeps projects on track and maximizes benefits.
Sustainability Performance target relating to environmental, social, and governance impacts. Every project impacts the environment, and sustainability performance targets must be set.
Benefits Performance target ensuring the project's outcomes are achieved and planned benefits are realized. The project manager ensures benefits are delivered.
