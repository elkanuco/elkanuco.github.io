Stakeholder Categories

Term Definition
Stakeholder Any individual, group, or organization that can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves to be affected by the project. May be internal or external to the organization.
Business Stakeholder Stakeholders who commission the work and ensure value for money. They fund the project and are concerned with return on investment. Represented by the executive on the project board.
User Stakeholder Those who will use or be impacted by the project's products. They help define requirements and ensure products meet their needs. Represented by the senior user role.
Supplier Stakeholder Those providing skills, knowledge, and resources to create products. May be internal departments or external organizations. Represented by the senior supplier role.
Stakeholder Engagement The process of identifying, analyzing, and managing stakeholder expectations and involvement throughout the project lifecycle.

Project Board Roles

Term Definition
Project Board The group accountable for project success and authorized to direct the project within limits set by the business. Comprises executive, senior user(s), and senior supplier(s).
Executive Single individual with ultimate responsibility for the project. Chairs the project board, ensures continued business justification, and represents business interests. Cannot be delegated or shared.
Senior User Board member ensuring user needs are specified and delivered products meet those needs. Responsible for user acceptance and benefits realization commitment.
Senior Supplier Board member providing supplier perspective, ensuring technical feasibility and quality. Responsible for providing resources and expertise to create products.

Management Roles

Term Definition
Project Manager Responsible for daily project management within board-established constraints. Manages stages, assigns work packages, monitors progress, and handles issues.
Team Manager Coordinates work to deliver one or more specialist products. Accepts work packages from project manager and manages team activities to complete them.
Project Support Provides administrative assistance to the project manager. May help with documentation, progress tracking, and cross-ecosystem integration. Must remain independent from assurance activities.
Project Assurance Project board's responsibility to monitor that the project remains viable and on track. Can be delegated but board retains accountability. Must be independent from project manager.
Change Authority Person or group delegated by the project board to approve specified changes within defined limits, reducing board workload for routine change decisions.

Management Layers

Term Definition
Business Layer Organizational level external to the project that commissions the work. Provides the mandate, appoints the executive, sets project tolerances, and authorizes tolerance breaches.
Directing Management layer where the project board makes key decisions at stage boundaries and manages by exception within established tolerances.
Managing Layer where the project manager handles day-to-day activities, assigns work, monitors progress, manages issues and risks, and reports to the board.
Delivering Layer where team managers and members execute work packages and create products within constraints established by the project manager.

People-Focused Concepts

Term Definition
Leadership Inspiring and motivating people to achieve objectives through collaboration, influence, and relationship management. Focuses on the "why" and creates shared vision.
Management Organizing and directing task execution according to agreed processes. Focuses on the "how" and ensures work follows established methods.
Co-creation Collaborative approach involving stakeholders in designing products and ways of working to ensure adoption and alignment across the project ecosystem.
Cultural Intelligence Ability to work effectively across different organizational cultures and contexts. Essential for leading beyond direct authority and across organizational boundaries.
Project Culture Shared attitudes, values, and working practices within the project team. Initially reflects organizational culture but may develop distinct characteristics.
Collaboration Working together across the project ecosystem to achieve shared objectives. Essential for successful project delivery in complex environments.

Communication & Change

Term Definition
Communication Management Approach Defines how project communications will be conducted, including methods, frequency, audiences, and responsibilities. Should emphasize listening as well as broadcasting.
Change Management Approach Describes the target organizational state and how the transition from current to target state will be managed, including interim states and enabling activities.
Organizational Ecosystem The complex network of relationships, structures, and cultures within the broader organization that the project must understand and navigate.
Project Ecosystem The temporary organizational structure created for the project, including all people, relationships, and ways of working established to deliver objectives.
Ways of Working Agreed processes, practices, and behaviors that define how the project team will operate. Most effective when co-created with team members.

Organizational Structure

Term Definition
Project Management Team All individuals with project management responsibilities, including project board, project manager, team managers, and any assurance or support roles.
Project Management Team Structure Diagram showing roles, responsibilities, and reporting relationships for project management. Forms part of the project initiation documentation.
Role Description Document defining specific responsibilities and authorities for each project management role, including accountability for performance targets like sustainability.
Work Breakdown Structure Hierarchical representation of work required during the project. Particularly useful when multiple work packages need coordination.
Organizational Design Process of structuring the project team and ways of working to achieve objectives. Includes understanding ecosystems, designing structure, developing relationships, and managing transitions.
Team Development Ongoing activities to build team capabilities, strengthen relationships, and adapt to changing project needs throughout the lifecycle.
