⚙️ What is Scrum?

Definition

Scrum is a lightweight framework that helps people, teams, and organizations generate value through adaptive solutions for complex problems. It is founded on empiricism and lean thinking.

Scrum Theory

Scrum employs an iterative, incremental approach to optimize predictability and control risk. It is based on three pillars: Transparency, Inspection, and Adaptation.

  • Transparency: Important aspects of the process must be visible to those performing the work.
  • Inspection: Scrum requires frequent inspection to detect undesirable variances.
  • Adaptation: Adjustments must be made as soon as possible to minimize deviation.

Benefits

Scrum removes ambiguities, enables quick releases, motivates teams, and supports adaptation to change. It reduces waste and fosters continuous improvement.

Purpose

Scrum was developed in the early 1990s. The Scrum Guide (© 2020 Ken Schwaber and Jeff Sutherland) defines it to help teams worldwide understand and apply it effectively.
